Tiny Feet Climate Action Planning (CAP)

Toolkit for Communities

Project Description

The goal of the Tiny Feet Toolkit is to help communities kickstart the Climate Action Plan (CAP) process. A CAP is a set of solutions and guidelines communities will follow to help reduce their carbon footprint. The initial phase of the standard CAP development process is an assessment of the greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions sources in the community, known as an emissions inventory. With the process requiring arduous source data collection and general understanding of climate science, the cost to perform this initial step inhibits many communities from pursuing climate action. In addition, the time spent performing an emissions inventory delays any environmental improvement recognized through action implementation. As a result, the Tiny Feet team created a toolkit for communities to access an estimation of their GHG emissions calculated from free public data. The Tiny Feet team encourages communities to use the toolkit to help ease their path in creating their CAP.

*Disclaimer* The data displayed is an estimation intended to help user in the initiating their CAP process. The information provided by the toolkit is NOT a comprehensive emissions inventory for the selected area.

Why is creating a community Climate Action Plan important in reducing global warming?

The UN Environment Program and the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) advise that global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions need to be cut by over half by 2030 in order to keep warming below a 1.5° C threshold, a critical tipping point that if exceeded would have catastrophic consequences to all planetary systems. The primary source of GHG emissions is the burning of fossil fuels to generate electricity, create heat, and run vehicles. GHG are also generated from the decomposition of waste in landfills. Climate action plans are roadmaps that enable communities to reduce their GHG emissions in ways best suited to local needs and behaviors.Internationally municipalities are committing to climate responsibility; in 2019, 77 countries and over 100 cities committed to net-zero carbon emissions by 2050 at the international Climate Action Summit. Unfortunately, the current commitments by cities and countries to net-zero emissions only get us about halfway to the emissions reductions necessary. This means that to best our chances for limiting the effects of global warming, CAPs need to increase drastically both in number and in effectivity. Simultaneously the effects of climate change and inequity are demanding immediate attention which GHG emissions reductions goals alone often fail to prioritize.

CO2e: Carbon Dioxide Equivalent, the standard unit for measurement of greenhouse gasses

User Description

The toolkit is designed to allow the users to select the county, city, or zip-code of interest within Colorado. The data is available in three different views. The first view is a map showing the average GHG emissions per person in each municipality. The second view allows users to input their county, city, or zip to display their emissions data per sector in chart form. The final view is a table consisting of the full dataset of calculated GHG emissions from each sector for a selected county, city, or zip code. The data shows the three major sectors in GHG emissions, Energy, Transportation, and Waste. The user can then press the recommendations link to look at a list of solutions in decreasing their GHG emissions. The recommendations list can also be filtered by societal co-benefits created by particular actions.
